Send us an article. Here's how.

If you are interested in submitting a feature, article, or blurb to Breakthrough, send a draft to tuftsresearch@gmail.com along with you name, major, and class to be considered for publishing. We will try to publish everything we get, but sometimes it may have to wait if our current issue is full. We will contact you once edits have been made by our staff for you to make any final changes before publishing your submission.

Features:

  • Based on Tufts research by students or faculty (including summer or study abroad stuff)
  • Involves reading recent scientific publications for background
  • Usually involves informally interviewing your research subject (~30 minutes)
  • 800-1000 words (2 pages in magazine)
  • Ideally with good pictures of lab work, etc.

Articles:

  • Can be on anything in any writing style/format but ideally still Tufts/Boston related
  • 600-750 words (1 â€“ 1.5 pages in magazine)

Blurbs:

  • Can be about whatever you want
  • 150-250 words (0.5 pages)

Potential Interview Questions:

  • What made you interested in this topic? What excites you about the research you are doing?
  • How do you go about investigating your interests? How to you choose which theories to apply or which experiments to start with?
  • What do you think makes someone a good researcher?
  • What is the most challenging aspect of your research?
  • What other areas of research could your work influence?
  • What will you be investigating next?

Formatting:

  • Cite sources with a number in parentheses (1) in the body of your submission. Citations should be in PLOS ONE format at the end of the article.
  • Footnotes DO NOT translate well to our editing software. Please do not use them.
